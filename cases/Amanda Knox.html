The 2011 film "Amanda Knox: Murder on Trial in Italy" is a made-for-TV movie that dramatizes the true story of Amanda Knox, an American exchange student who was accused of murdering her British roommate Meredith Kercher in Italy in 2007. The film stars Marisa Pavan as Amanda Knox and Rachael Leigh Cook as Meredith Kercher. It follows the events leading up to the murder, the investigation, and the subsequent trial, which was widely covered by the media. The film portrays the complex and often sensationalized case, including the accusations of satanic ritual and the media frenzy surrounding Knox's appearance and behavior. It also explores the relationships between Knox, Kercher, and their other roommates, as well as the investigation led by Italian police. The film was directed by Mike Robe and written by Robe and Andrea Newman. It premiered on Lifetime Television in the United States on November 21, 2011. Please note that this film is a dramatization and not a documentary, and some artistic liberties were taken to enhance the storytelling.
