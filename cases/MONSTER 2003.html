The film is based on the true story of Aileen Wuornos, a former prostitute who became one of America's first female serial killers, murdering seven men between 1989 and 1990 before being executed in Florida in 2002. The film stars Charlize Theron in an acclaimed performance as Wuornos, earning her the Academy Award for Best Actress, the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Drama, and numerous other accolades. Christina Ricci portrays Selby Wall, a fictionalized version of Wuornos's real-life girlfriend, Tyria Moore. The story focuses on the nine-month period between 1989 and 1990, during which Wuornos meets Selby Wall in a gay bar in Daytona Beach, Florida, and begins a relationship that offers her a fleeting sense of love and hope. After being brutally raped and beaten by a client, Vincent Corey, Wuornos kills him in self-defense and begins a killing spree, justifying her actions as protection against predatory men. As her crimes escalate, her relationship with Selby deteriorates under the weight of her actions and the growing danger, culminating in Wuornos's arrest, trial, and eventual death sentence.
