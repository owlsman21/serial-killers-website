Dennis Rader, known as the BTK Killer — short for Bind, Torture, Kill — terrorized Wichita, Kansas from 1974 to 1991. He murdered ten people while maintaining a façade as a church president, husband, and city employee. 🩸 Featured Crime Case: Dennis Rader — The BTK Killer
📅 Active Years: 1974–1991
📍 Location: Wichita, Kansas
🔪 Confirmed Victims: 10
🧠 Modus Operandi: Home invasions, strangulation, binding victims, taunting police with letters
🔥 Key Events
- Sent detailed letters to media and police, describing his crimes
- Stopped killing for years, then resurfaced in 2004
- Sent a floppy disk to police — metadata led to his church computer
⚖️ Arrest and Trial
- Arrested in 2005 after digital forensics cracked his identity
- Pleaded guilty to all 10 murders
- Sentenced to 10 consecutive life terms
🧠 Psychological Profile
Rader exhibited narcissistic and sadistic traits, craving control and recognition. His double life stunned the community — a killer hiding in plain sight. 📰 Legacy
The BTK case remains a landmark in criminal profiling and digital forensics. Rader’s own writings and courtroom confessions offer chilling insight into his psyche.
